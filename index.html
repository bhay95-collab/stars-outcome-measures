Rivermead Post-Concussion Symptoms Questionnaire
16 items rating severity of post-concussion symptoms compared to before injury. Rated 0–4.
King 1995
Instructions to patient: "For each symptom below, please rate how much it has affected you in the last 24 hours compared to before the head injury."
0 = Not experienced at all · 1 = No more of a problem than before · 2 = Mild problem · 3 = Moderate problem · 4 = Severe problem

—/12

Subscales (Eyres 2005): RPQ-3 (items 1–3: headache, dizziness, nausea) captures early symptom cluster (range 0–12). RPQ-13 (items 4–16) captures later, persisting symptoms (range 0–52). These are psychometrically more robust than the total score. Post-Concussion Syndrome indicator: >3 symptoms rated ≥2 at 3 months following mild head injury meets diagnostic criteria (King 1999). Test-retest reliability: RPQ-13 r=0.89, RPQ-3 r=0.72. Important: RPQ should not be used in isolation — high overlap with depression (BDI-II). Scores may reflect pre-existing conditions, pain, or mental health rather than concussion alone.

15-item patient-reported outcome measure of vision-related quality of life following stroke/ABI. Designed for all main types of post-stroke visual impairment.

Emerging measure note: The BIVI-IQ was published in 2019 and validated in a stroke population in 2023. It is the only PROM specifically designed for the full range of post-stroke visual impairment.
